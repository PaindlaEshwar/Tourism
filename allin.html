Favourite Places

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a stunning white marble mausoleum located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh India, on the southern bank of the Yamuna River. it's a symbol of eternal love and a masterpiece of Mughal architecture blending Indian, Persian, and Islamic styles

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is a breathtaking gurudwara (Sikh temple) in Amritsar, Punjab, India. It's a symbol of equality, peace, and spirituality, attracting millions of visitors worldwide.

Ramappa Temple

Ramappa Temple, also known as Rudreshwara Temple, is a stunning example of Kakatiya architecture located in Palampet village, Telangana, India. This 13th-century temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is renowned for its intricate carvings, innovative construction techniques, and historical significance.

Varanasi Temple

Varanasi, a city in Uttar Pradesh, India, is renowned for its rich spiritual heritage and is home to numerous ancient and revered temples. Here's an overview of some prominent temples.Varanasi Temple is most famo Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Mysore Palace

Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is a stunning example of Indo-Saracenic architecture located in Mysore, Karnataka, India. This historic palace was built between 1897 and 1912 for the Wadiyar dynasty, who ruled Mysore for over 600 years.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 for being "the jewel of Islamic art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage". It is regarded as one of the best examples of Mughal architecture and a symbol of Indian history. The Taj Mahal is a major tourist attraction and attracts more than five million visitors a year. In 2007, it was declared a winner of the New 7 Wonders of the World initiative. The Taj Mahal and its setting, surrounding grounds, and structures are a Monument of National Importance, administered by the Archaeological Survey of India.
